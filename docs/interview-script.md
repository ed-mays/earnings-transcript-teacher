# Discovery Interview Script

**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Target:** Aspiring financial readers who want to start reading earnings calls but find them intimidating.
**Where to find them:** r/investing commenters asking beginner questions, personal/professional network, finance Discord servers.

Record their **exact words** — paraphrase loses the signal.

---

## Warm-up (2 min)

- "How do you currently learn about the companies you invest in?"
- "Have you ever tried reading an earnings call transcript?"
  - If yes: "What was that like?"
  - If no: "What's stopped you?"

## Pain exploration (5 min)

- "What's confusing or intimidating about earnings calls?"
- "What did you wish someone would explain?"
- "When you hit something you don't understand, what do you do?" (Google it, ask Reddit, skip it, give up?)
- "How do you decide if a company is doing well or poorly right now?"

## Solution validation (5 min)

- "Imagine a tool that takes a real earnings call and walks you through it — highlights the important parts, explains the jargon, and lets you ask questions about anything you don't understand. Would you try it?"
- "What would make you come back a second time?"
- "Would you pay for something like that? What do you think it's worth?"
- "Would you prefer to learn a general skill (how to read any earnings call) or get help with specific calls each quarter?"

## Close (3 min)

- "Is there anything else about learning to read earnings calls that we haven't talked about?"
- "Can I follow up when the tool is ready for beta testers?"
- Get contact info if they're interested.

---

## After the interview

- Write up notes within 1 hour while memory is fresh
- Highlight exact quotes that reveal pain or desire
- Note whether they match the target user profile (aspiring reader, not experienced analyst)
- After 3 interviews: look for patterns in the exact words they used
