# First Substack Post — Draft Structure

**Purpose:** Teach something real from a transcript. Tease the tool. Get subscribers and beta interest.
**Adapt for:** X/Twitter thread (shorter), finance Discords (conversational), Reddit (week 2-3, no links).

---

## Post structure

### 1. Hook: Why earnings calls are intimidating

Open with the experience of trying to read an earnings call for the first time. What it feels like. Why most people bounce off them. Make the reader feel seen — "yes, that's exactly my experience."

### 2. The teachable moment: ONE concept from a real transcript

Pick one passage from the transcript you dogfooded. Show:
- **The actual quote** from the CEO/CFO (blockquote it)
- **What an aspiring reader sees:** jargon, numbers, vague language
- **What's actually being said:** decode it in plain English
- **The "aha":** what this tells you about the company that you can't get from a headline

This is the Feynman technique in action — and it's the core value prop of EarningsFluency.

### 3. The build-in-public angle

- "I'm building a tool that does this interactively"
- What it does: takes real transcripts, highlights what matters, explains jargon, lets you ask questions
- Why you're building it: you learned to read earnings calls and it changed how you invest, but the learning curve was brutal
- Where it is: early, working prototype, looking for beta testers
- Be honest and specific — build-in-public audiences reward transparency

### 4. Call to action

- "If you want to try it when it's ready, subscribe to this newsletter"
- "Reply to this email or DM me — I'd love to hear what confuses you most about earnings calls"
- The reply/DM request doubles as user discovery (Step 3 of the issue)

---

## Adaptation notes

### For X/Twitter thread
- Lead with the decoded quote (the teachable moment) — that's the hook on X
- 4-5 tweets: quote → decode → what it means → "I'm building a tool for this" → CTA
- Link to the Substack post in the last tweet

### For finance Discords
- More conversational tone
- Lead with a question: "Has anyone here tried reading an actual earnings call transcript?"
- Share the teachable moment as a reply/follow-up
- Mention the tool casually: "I've been building something that helps with this"

### For Reddit (week 2-3)
- **No links, no CTA, no self-promotion**
- Pure teaching: "Here's what I learned reading [TICKER]'s earnings call"
- The tool only comes up if someone asks "how did you learn this?"
- Shorter than Substack — Reddit rewards concise, opinionated posts

---

## Graduation observation (track while writing)

As you write this post, notice: is the value coming from teaching a transferable SKILL (how to decode executive language) or from applying that skill to NEW DATA (what Apple said this quarter)?

After 4 posts, report which pattern dominates. This determines course vs. subscription model.
