## Welcome Post (Proposed)

**Welcome to Beyond the Tutorial**

*For developers ready to build something real.*

***

If you've ever finished a tutorial, felt like you understood it, and then stared at a blank editor wondering why you still couldn't build anything that worked outside the example — this publication is for you.

Tutorials are excellent at teaching syntax. They are not excellent at teaching you what happens when you add real users, a deployment pipeline, test coverage, authentication, cost constraints, and the pressure of something that actually has to work. That gap — between the toy example and the production system — is where most developers get stuck. It's also where the most interesting learning happens.

*Beyond the Tutorial* is where I write about that gap.

***

**What This Is**

I'm Ed Mays — technology consultant and mentor with thirty years of experience in production software. Over the past three months I've been building the **Earnings Transcript Teacher**: an AI-native application that helps retail investors learn to analyze earnings calls. I started with no Python background, no NLP experience, no knowledge of LLM APIs, and no prior experience shipping with AI-assisted coding tools.

Almost nothing I needed to know was covered in the tutorials. This publication is my attempt to write the resource I wished had existed.

***

**What to Expect**

Honest, grounded writing about what real-world software development actually looks like — with a current focus on AI-assisted development, LLMs in production, and the product thinking that surrounds them. One article every one to two weeks. No filler. No hype.

If something I write saves you a day of debugging, a bad architectural decision, or the particular frustration of realizing a tutorial left you completely unprepared — that's the goal.
